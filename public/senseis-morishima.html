Morishima Sensei moved several times before settling in his current residence in Miyamae Ward, Kawasaki City.

At the age of seven, he began practicing Kendo at a local dojo.

At the recommendation of the dojo's head instructor, Yasuo Shimekake Sensei, he started Iaido at the age of nine and Jodo at the age of eleven, continuing all three disciplines at that dojo until he was eighteen.

Later, upon entering university, he left Mr. Shimekake's dojo and joined the Shinbukan Ishido Dojo, where he began practicing Kendo, Iaido, and Jodo under the strict guidance of Ishido Shizufumi Sensei. At that time, he held 3rd Dan ranks in all three disciplines.

He studied Muso Shinden Jushin-ryu and Muso Shinden-ryu Iaido under Ishido Sensei. After graduating from university and becoming a working adult, his job became very busy, and he stopped practicing Kendo and Jodo after obtaining 5th Dan in both disciplines.

He continued practicing both styles of Iaido, obtaining 6th Dan at the age of 27 and 7th Dan at the age of 33. Then, under the intense, broad, and enthusiastic guidance of Ishido Sensei, he received his Iaido 8th Dan at the age of 46.

He also participated in the All Japan Iaido Tournament a total of 20 times as a representative of Kanagawa Prefecture, from the 5th Dan division through the 7th Dan division.

Among those results, he placed 2nd twice in the 5th Dan division, 1st once and 2nd once in the 6th Dan division, and 1st once plus 2nd four times in the 7th Dan division.

Regarding those achievements, he said that he owes everything to Ishido Sensei's guidance and encouragement, and that he is deeply grateful.

In the future, he intends to dedicate himself to further study and contribute to the development of Iaido.
