Takizawa Tomomi Sensei

Name
Takizawa Tomomi
Born
March 1982, Gunma Prefecture (current residence)
Iaido
Kyoshi 7th Dan

In 2000, Takizawa Sensei entered Ritsumeikan University in Kyoto. At the invitation of a friend, she joined the university's Iaido club in June 2000, beginning her journey in Iaido. She met Kyoshi 7th Dan Yuko Inari during her university years, and they practiced together frequently from their student days onward.

From her student days in Kyoto, she studied Hoki-ryu under the guidance of Kyoshi 8th Dan Motoshi Yamada. During her university years, she became runner-up at the 2002 All Japan Student Iaido Tournament. The winner was Kyoshi 7th Dan Nobukazu Onishi, and third place went to Kyoshi 7th Dan Beau Brier.

Around the time of her university graduation, she became a disciple of Hanshi 8th Dan Tsuyoshi Yasunaga in Kumamoto Prefecture. She traveled from Kyoto to Kumamoto Prefecture almost every month after work to receive instruction.

In April 2017, she returned to her hometown of Gunma Prefecture and established the Hoki-ryu Iaido Haruna-kai. Currently, she trains with members mainly in their 20s and 30s, and regularly attends the Shinbukan Ishido Dojo after work, striving to hone her skills every day.

She obtained her 7th Dan in November 2021 and Kyoshi rank in November 2023.

Since participating in a seminar in Stevenage, England in 2015, where she won the 6th Dan division, she has regularly participated in seminars in Europe. She has also taken part in seminars in Australia four times so far.

Her participation in European seminars includes Germany in 2017, the Netherlands in 2019, Poland in 2024, Hamburg in 2025, and Mulhouse, France in 2026.

Major achievements include 13 consecutive wins in the 4th to 6th Dan division at the All Japan Iaido Kagawa Tournament, 8 consecutive wins at the All Japan Iaido Kochi Tournament, 3 consecutive wins at the Osaka Iaido Tournament, and victories at other tournaments throughout Japan.

At the All Japan Iaido Tournament, she achieved Top 8 once in the 5th Dan division, Top 4 once in the 6th Dan division, and Top 4 twice in the 7th Dan division.

Takizawa Sensei continues to combine serious technical study with active international participation, contributing to seminars in Japan, Europe, and Australia while developing the next generation of practitioners through her work in Gunma and at the Shinbukan Ishido Dojo.
