Otake Sensei graduated from the Department of Agricultural Chemistry, Faculty of Agriculture, Tokyo University of Agriculture in April 1979.

He then worked at Komazawa Gakuen Girls' Junior & Senior High School, teaching junior high science and high school biology from 1979 to 2019.

He later took on a professional teaching role in Jodo at Kokushikan and Tokai universities, where he currently works.

His study of martial arts began with Kendo at the age of 11 in Kishiya Dojo, and six years later he started learning Jodo at Genbukan Sakaue Dojo in Tsurumi. One year later, in the same dojo, he also started learning Iaido.

He studied Jodo under Yano Shoichiro Sensei, Hanshi 8th Dan and direct student of Otofuji Sensei. When starting under Yano Sensei, he first visited the dojo for a trial session and trained for an hour and a half. Afterward, he purchased the jo that he had used, which was a brand-new item for sale, and began regular training the following week.

He studied Iaido under the late Hirokazu Sakagami, head of the Genbukan, practicing the Muso Jikiden Eishin-ryu style after being advised to do so because his handling of the tachi during a Jodo grading examination was poor.

During the following years he delivered impressive performances at National Jodo Championship level, winning gold at least once in all divisions from 3rd to 7th Dan except 4th Dan, and he also won a bronze medal at 6th Dan in the All-Japan Invitational Iaido Tournament (Multi-School).

Alongside a variety of official management and administration roles in Kanagawa, he has been a member of the ZNKR Jodo Committee since 2011 and has performed in the official Jodo demonstration in Kyoto for two years. He is also a frequent grading panelist on a national basis.

In Europe he is also widely known for his contribution to coaching Budo overseas. He first visited the UK in 1982 to teach at the BKA Jodo and Iaido Seminar. This continued until 1984, during which time he was instrumental in developing Jodo in Europe alongside Hiroi Sensei and Ishido Sensei.

He then began to visit and teach in the UK every year from 2004 to 2016, missing only two years during that time. From 2016 he began teaching in the Netherlands at their Summer Iaido and Jodo seminar and continues to visit on a nearly annual basis. He has also taught in the USA, Poland, France, Canada, and frequently in Australia.

Additionally, he has been selected to be an official delegation member for the European Jodo Championships. Those fortunate enough to train under him will recognize the superior teaching skills that come from his long years as a professional teacher and lifelong Budo enthusiast.
